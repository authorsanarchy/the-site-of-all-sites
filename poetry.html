Losers

By Lizard

Pines poke against the chalk line of a setting sky.
Seats, striated muscle fibres, move as one. The machine moves as one.
A hushed din sets over its bodies, pressed together in fear of the world’s weight. Cloud-veins pulse, things pause, and satisfaction strings suspended between sweaty heads.
They’re all losers, the lot. All having lost—
But this nears the land of living hope, or so the church board says, and once they find the door to it, they’ll stop waiting for another minute; another hour; another delay, wheels rolling down the hill, snow gunking the gears— an accelerative paradox wherein time slips ahead but sits perfectly still and
Waits for them to notice.

Shower

By Lizard

Ten fingers drumming on a head’s crown,
Falling to sheets of seeds of spread-out second skin
On the splintered slant of a needle-tip,
Melting into beads that gather the skin ‘till it folds neatly as lace.
Water is touch.
Water is quiet and hoarse and a
Mother who loves you rough —
Whose arms bar men but not the stiff of her own screams.
Water takes many bodies.
Water begs a certain nakedness to truly get at the matter
And play time back again, as recorded by the stream, screening
Pearls and fingertips every evening.

Texas

By Lizard

She is eighty-six years old;
Or at least I think so. And she grows flowers, these gorgeous red mums and purple hyacinths, but damn anyone who says those two colours don’t match.
She’s got a fiery spirit. She kills at bingo, reminds everyone to turn the lights off on Earth Day. I’ve never heard a louder whoop than hers. She keeps her porch clean, hands full with yesterday’s work, because every morning— porch-sitting time, watching-paint-dry time— shaves one month off her face. Oh, yes. And she thumbs her chips rarely. There’s nothing more than what you’re given, she says, until Newton’s Laws take hold and it’s all gone from there.
She is eighty-six years old and much younger than me.

The window to the soul is lexically limited

By Lizard

I did not think anything phantasmagorically giant, epistemologically relevant.
My head slipped past rhymes and big words.
In the moment, eyes glued upward, I only thought:
Bright. Bright. More white than yellow— why does every kid draw them yellow?
They are space that I should only take up for a time.
Any longer and I’ll disappear, poof, without colour. Without even footprints on the wood.
12:45, round time. Without even footprints.
My arms were rock, head a bullet casing, and the brain inside strawberry jelly.
Not once did I think: “I should write about this.
If I write now, this moment will never go grey.” No. Never.
Instead I thought about sleep and my cold little toe,
Held like a beacon over the sea, pith of summer’s end.

Health

By Lizard

I hauled my legs, all sinew and tear, to the very peak Where I grappled with fate and told myself the meaning of “push”— not a force, but more so a feeling
Like home
Then I watched the sun dance a glittering tap over the hills and I too, became diamond
Once the good thing passed, I gathered my wares, spare as the carved stone footholds,
Tongue swollen, bleeding,
heart weary, and made a careful way down with my shovel and axe How good of a miracle it is to stand — or perhaps not a miracle Some worlds away, I knew my worth, my world’s preparations But I had long forgotten them.
I was compelled to indict my name in the cold, hard mountain edge and let my body sweep along the stream, let someone find me bloated and gone,
Until darkness came barreling forth like an angry charge, ten thousand men at the door told me of the time, told me of myself and the sorry state I was in After which I rose,
Alive and open, to cut words from their shells, face the force that moves air —
In fact, look straight and tell the boldest lie: No worries.

By Lizard

When they met again, it wasn’t quite right.
Untuned voices facing the wall,
They spoke like priests
With a vital sermon:
Praise, praise, praise,

Accomplishments are on the horizon if only
You’d listen—
But there was a hardness to get past.
One had children, twins;
Another had tenure, papers.
Someone from way back when was dead,
Rolling in his unmarked, unmentioned grave so violently
You’d just have to skip over his hole in time.
Ten years ago, things seemed so

Evil Wizard’s Evil Pupils

By Lizard

Most young mages strive for the topmost rung of the ladder. That rung is a dream, and was always meant to be a dream.
When you’re really there, the view from the sky gets old; watching armies you’ve raised, villages you will raze, and wondering with the last shred of goodness you have left,
“What now?”
Success isn’t what they chalk it up to be.
It’ll make sense when you’re older, the great wizards say, but remember; they also say the mind is all that needs feeding.

I’m sorry I’m a freak

By Lizard

These shelves reek of death.
It’s as good as coffining, to wrap pages in such a way with
No headstone for polite measure—
You wouldn’t treat criminals this badly.
You’d give them room for their words to sink in, perfuse the air,
Breathe life and tone into rhyme.
‘Round here, violent titles are shut up.
The strategic arrangement of syntax matters that much more when
Letters are all the dignity they have left,
Weapons no longer chemistry or sex or waking memory but being—
Being out in the open, of which they stay deprived.
Kill him. No, indite him!
Flatten him ten times down and let desks, bare flashes, draw meaning like
Dry-rotted string from the bricks of this place.
Keep him in cells so narrow only the purpose-toward dare go near
And even they walk quickly, like hunted rabbits,
Eyes peeled for a crack or
Show?

Out This Place

By Lizard

It’s a sun-bleached day. We stomp on the boardwalk as if to break its neck. The wood springs back and sand comes flying at our faces, burning our eyes, all over our sandals. I smell like aldehydes and coconut balm. You smell like me; we share the stuff.
Jewels are in our eyes, sparkles in our sea, consumption in our flushed red cheeks. Lunch is a plastic bag I throw to you. You throw it back, since none of us want bad news. We’ve collected enough shells to build a castle, and that’ll be enough for food.
The tide takes long drags of shore-smoke. We watch the beach houses groan, fronts broken off. Signs that might have said “Lifeguard” or “OPEN” litter their bodies. Erosion’s one hell of a killer.
When red spills into the sky, my hands are raisins and your hair is tangled every which way. I suggest sleeping here, on our secret swath of beach where the tourists dare not step, but you drag me home.
You’ve always been serious like that. Always worried about things I can’t see. I’m blind from exposure, blind from my future, but this place is heaven
And the good need no eyes to see their purity.

New Name

By Lizard

To relinquish a name is never to kill the thought.
Names are but words, and words do not liberate a thing.
They force obscurity onto what, in the mind’s eye, appears obvious—
Sounds more concrete than the crooked bridge of a nose run awry,
Where little cars could race if blown up in size, or
Curves twisting a tender vine ‘round o then m then swollen, soft c.
We find no title for what grows
Between dandelion fluff and two lawn chairs,
Two bodies spread red and eagle under the burning sun.
In fact, we have long since tossed it away.
Like pioneers, we laud what comes from earth and
Dirt, the occasional crooked-toothed smile, shapes our freedom.

Easy Emigree

By Lizard

It’s easy,
Choking on words in the windpipe,
Weighed down with what you need but haven’t the translator for.
On your sickbed, you won’t mince anything.
You’ll revert, as elders turn children and children need mothers.
The wonted tongue—poetry, rhymes, prayers—
Will come shooting from your death rattle like shrapnel.
You’ve dodged war to get here, sacrificed even speech,
But God knows that’s the easy part;
What kills you is to leave.

Worship

By Lizard

Shy morning. Quiet spiderweb spool and gem-red berry clusters keen;
Then, in a crackling cough,
The sky claps, awash with grey.
Honey-hearted amanita subsume the soil, loan to bear navigators
Knowing the proper way that is
Gladness and particular, constant joy. Easy keeping.
One must avoid dark lakes and ward direction by
Stars always, and lose shadow
Light on foot.
So she speaks:
The meek shall inherit the earth—
My pines, sugar maples, wolves whose ribs and eyes are hollow —
For they cannot be tamed but
Kept in solemn mind.
I entrust only my shyest.

Daycare

By Lizard

“Imagine life is a big hill and we’re all speeding down in cars.
Really fast. I want to crash off a cliff and be fine afterward”.
“What’s at the bottom of the cliff? Why’s it so special?”
“Dunno”.
Sun on sweat-stained oil slicks,
Air both dry and sticky to the tongue,
Two boys could not see what lay ahead.
Grinding their studded bikes, they paused;
“I think we’re going the wrong way”.
Try and try as we might, the bottom is unclear.
I conceive it to be the very end, or the last frontier of being.
What is dignity if not
Expecting heaven and receiving fishbowls on the freeway?
Through this seaglass frosted like winter,
Cars reel as movie scenes, and they all stare.
We are delirious and heated and happy.
The feeling, “fun good”.

English

By Lizard

I met her today.
Instead of proselytising like I’d hoped,

She told me to drink more water.
I dry heaved my soul over the carpet.
She wove bits of chicken and things into gold,
Roped paper ‘round my neck;
Led me gently, as worshippers must be put off and away
Into the closed-off hall, then spoke even gentler:
This was where I wrote the world. This is why Babel fell on bad engineering.
There is nobody, really, to stop me from getting any larger
And you—
Here she clapped me over the shoulder, I watched storms raise their hackles—
Wouldn’t dare try.

Mirror

By Lizard

18 yr. old female visits 18 yr. old male.
A thin spread of dust settles over her glasses, reflecting divots and ruched shells of light.
For an unusual moment she sees two 18. yr old males,
But there is only one.
His half-lidded eyes trace hers curiously, almost disappointedly,
Like he expected his people and hoped she might bring them.
Maybe then she is a show and he observes her, or thinks he does,
Or has been told he does.

Observer: So the wild, the wild. Have you seen the wild, the palm oil, your dead mother?
Observed: No, though how I wish. I’d rather be there, shot and bled out, than scratching my ear for you.

A crop of burnt hair fans his cheek.
This soft look, this hopelessly wise look, comes about him, and
She regrets having imagined any rudeness from his mouth.
Maybe then he is kind and innocent and still not hateful.
Maybe she is hateful.
Following that thought, he holds a cardboard square to his head.
She laughs.
If they have anything in common, it is captivity;
Ideally both would escape and return to fanfare,
Hearty tall families.

Christmas (wellness)

By Lizard

Fog settles over the road.
I can almost make out the smokestacks and thornbush trees that peek out from behind my house-line,
And the crooked street sign
Marking where black pavement jump cuts to grey.
This is a harsh divide, something I can’t place,
But I suppose we all go through it eventually — usually before eighteen.
Most of us survive, complacent or otherwise,
No matter the fact
And besides the odds, which are often wrong.

Madman’s last words before
ethical electrocution

By Lizard

“Scientists say that life is a bitter race between us and them. Find us over purled clouds and passion pulled tight. Find them rowing twenty miles from shore, escaping. Linguists say that “escape” collects the dead old bones of Vulgar Latin, not to be confused with fire — not to be confused with escaliers de secours, where the protest-children of dead languages bide their time. Right, did you know? Psychologists say that children learn to kill from mom and dad. It’s a theory. Stimulus, cognition, behaviour, in a mediating environment, create us — and them. And cosmologists too. Cosmologists say that words are the second-best creators. Words may help you escape, set the roving fire, maybe even report you to the in and out-groups— but the cosmos, an eschatological mess… The cosmos do you right.
I don’t want to be a protest-child. I want to be a particle. I’ve learned murder and language and cognitive psychology and all the pain of a breaking body and yet, for this, I cannot write poetry. Poets say that passion comes from somewhere secret; a slice of space, a sprig of sage or lonely love. They always, always say it’s us, the poets, against the bitter human race. How sweet I am still, to disagree with all my heart.”

Excerpt from “Totally
Normal Alien Retreat”

By Lizard

If a human being “doesn’t want to live like this”, “like this” means “on Earth”.
On Earth, there will never be an acceptable standard of change; no end to the unwelcoming.
Perhaps a million years ago we all lived in one place— I imagine happiness pouring over into enough bowls for everybody— but these days, we’ve spread so far and so thin, like margarine on toast, that one can no longer find community-centric incentives to stay.

Girls in space, they like it better. It’s a whole galaxy open to mistakes. Never before was there so much canvas room for pent-up painters; fed-up fainters; weak constitutions who simply couldn’t convalesce by the sea any longer.
Plumbing the depths of their souls, you might find bereavement for Earth’s dusty old spot, but no regret. Never regret. Moondust loves girls and girls love moondust.

Ignorance

By Error

Sometimes I think I miss the days when ignorance was enough.
Before I felt like I might vomit at the sound of every bluff.
We bask in fleeting bliss like Icarus,
Before we drown under the weight of the world.

Sometimes I think I miss the days when good and bad was enough.
Before bad meant almost unthinkably vile and good meant the least corrupt.
Those days it still felt like people fought for the right stuff.
When justice, when goodness always had your back you didn’t need to be so tough.

Sometimes I think I miss the haze of those days,
When you could still cast your gaze other ways.
But soon enough the illusion take a graze,
And suddenly the whole world is a blaze.

We slip from the world of perfect rhyme,
And suddenly the whole world seems rotten,
And it’s been rotting the whole fucking time.

I don’t think I really miss the days when ignorance was enough.
Even when the days are rough,
And I wish I could just believe in the bluff,
At the end of the day I know I would rather know.

You need to know the status quo to look in down and say ‘Fuck No.’
You need to know that it’s not enough to slow or pine for days long ago.
The first step to changing anything is to first be willing to grow.

Burning Broken Beaker

By Error

Glass, it appears, is an excellent conductor of heat.
The lattice structure combined with a lack of an order allows heat to spread evenly.
That same lattice, as it turns out, also makes glass very brittle.
When part of the solid gets damaged, it dislodges other molecules causing it to crack.
Heat doesn't do much to help this, weakening the bonds between the molecules.
When one takes a glass beaker, and heats it up, then knocks over said hot glass beaker causing it to shatter, it creates a very hot, very sharp obstacle.
If one were to accidentally grab such an obstacle, it would almost certainly hurt, probably leaving burns.
I’m not contemplating this for any particular reason, but I will note that writing is rather a pain right now given the bandages wrapped around my thumb.
I don’t think I like chemistry class anymore.

The Sky in the Window

By Error

The room is wide and dull, filled with people packed in like beans.
Not talking, barely breathing, simply waiting with unease.
Four small windows line the walls, cloudy as can be.
A single small sliver of sky peaks into the desolate room.
A single small sliver of sky.
The only evidence of a world beyond this place.
A world where people are free to roam, not wracked by the crippling dread of sitting and waiting in this god awful room.
A world where people can run and play and scream, comforting noises reminding us of a world beyond, drowned about by the walls and the pain and the panic and the chaos when the waiting finally subsides, and all hell breaks loose in our minds.
But it ends. Eventually.
The panic doesn’t last forever, and the waiting dread is replaced by a comforting boredom and acceptance.
The doors open, and we leave free to the sky that’s still there waiting.
Then we do it all again tomorrow.

Warrior's Dance

By Error

We watch the display of a perfected art
The clash of a blade or the swish of a dart
With the blooding running hot through all the hearts
We watch as the warrior dance

Like an orchestra on its stage
This symphony of violence and of rage
The battles that we continue to wage
We watch as the warriors dance

Their movement speak like tales once told
Each one unique, and yet centuries old
Of fire, of pain, and of bitter cold
We watch as the warriors dance

As all things do, it comes to an end
With strength to recover, and wounds to mend
And to bury those who we called friend
We watch as the warriors mourn

I'm Still Here

By Error

Sometimes when I sit
I think of the things I've done.
The roads I could have taken
The fights I could have won.

If you had the the chance
Would you want to change a choice?
To try and do things better
Under different circumstance.

But if you change the things you’ve done
Just who does one become?
If we are defined by our past
Who are we if it doesn’t last?

I guess I am just dreaming
Contemplating “what if?”
Of a past that could shift.

But I don’t think I could
And I don’t think I should
I don’t even think I would.
Sorry for wasting your time.

Alone

By Error

Sometimes when I sit
They say home is where the heart is
But I lost mine long ago
I left it in some ditch I think.
Exactly where, I do not know.
If that is where my heart rest
Does that make it my home?
If I don’t know where I left it
Then I truly am alone.
Maybe if I keep looking
I'll find it again someday.
But until I find it
Lonely I shall stay.
